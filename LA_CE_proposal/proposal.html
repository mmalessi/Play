Project Roadmap

I’ve put together a roadmap of phases based on the key tasks that I gathered from meeting with Rachel. Here’s a little bit about how it’s organized.



  • Overview Each phase has multiple tasks related to it. I’ve also estimated the rough amount of time it will take to complete each phase.
  • Dates There are no actual dates associated with the timeline. Once I start working with LA and CE teams we can adapt the timeline to the calendar.
  • Flexibility As you know, no amount of planning can account for everything. Flexibility is crucial for creative projects—as we move through the phases, things are bound to fluctuate.
  • Overlap Many tasks and some phases can be completed at the same time—e.g., phase I and II can overlap. Meeting with the team might take two weeks because of scheduling, but in the meantime I can also begin other tasks.
  • Timing I’ve buffered each phase with extra time. E.g., phase one may only take 10 hours, but I’ve allotted 2 weeks to make sure we have plenty of time to schedule in those 10 hours.

Team Roles

Do I need a team? Nope, I have the experience to complete this entire project on my own. Would a team be helpful? Yes! If we want to get this project done faster. Team or no team, that’s up to you. But, if we think a team is a good idea… Who will I need on the team?

Leader

Who: That's Me!

Ownership & Charter: I will own most of the tasks of each phase. Lead the vision of the project. Interface and work closely with the CE and LA teams.


Coder

Who: TBD. I am looking to hire a Baltimore native (where I’m from) who is queer/trans/PoC. (Diversity on my team is very important). I have some non-Baltimore contacts who could definitely fill this role if I’m not able to find anyone.

I have the experience to complete this entire project on my own; however, to produce award winning work, in this time frame, bringing on another coder could be a smart move.

Budget

In order for this infographic to be easily adjustable by you, that means I have to build a front-end and back-end stack—i.e. an application. This aspect challenges my technological capacity and will be quite involved.

The rest of the project is manageable and at a rate of $50/hr the finished project will come out to between $2000-3000. Including the building of the application that budget could easily double—somewhere around $5000-8000.

Based on this, here are some packages you can consider.

MVP (minimum viable product) — $2000

  • An iframe of an interactive infographic that can be embedded by your teams onto your sites or elsewhere. What you’ll receive:
    • html, css, and javascript files (un-hosted)
  • Visuals and colors that are on brand with both LA and CE. You’ll received a shared Google Drive folder including:
    • a file indicating all colors and fonts used
    • png files for all the static images used
  • 0 revisions: I will work closely with both teams throughout the project but once it is completed I will make no further changes.
  • Timeframe: 2-4 weeks

MVP Iterated — $3000

  • An iframe of an interactive infographic that can be embedded by your teams onto your sites or elsewhere. What you’ll receive:
    • html, css, and javascript files (un-hosted)
  • Visuals and colors that are on brand with both LA and CE. You’ll received a shared Google Drive folder including:
    • a file indicating all colors and fonts used
    • png files for all the static images used
  • 1 revision: I will work closely with both teams throughout the project. Once I’ve completed the technical implementation, I will convene the stakeholders for a round of feedback and make changes accordingly.
  • Timeframe: 4 weeks
